Bridging Antimicrobial Resistance, the Gut–Brain Axis, and Regenerative Medicine

Anurag Tyagi  |  MS Zoology  |  4+ Years Research Experience  |  Molecular & Cellular Biology

My research spans three interconnected frontiers in biomedical science — development of monoclonal antibodies against multidrug-resistant pathogens, the gut–microbiome–brain axis in neurodegenerative disease, and bioengineered tissue constructs for regenerative medicine. Across all three, my focus is on translating mechanistic laboratory findings into therapeutically relevant insights.

Currently a Senior Research Fellow at the Translational Health Science and Technology Institute (THSTI), I work at the interface of immunology and microbiology to develop novel antibody-based strategies against MDR and XDR Klebsiella pneumoniae.

My scientific journey

My path into research began with a deep curiosity about life at the cellular and molecular level — sharpened through undergraduate training in Life Sciences at the University of Delhi and a Master's in Zoology at CCS University. Early on, I recognized that infectious disease, neurodegeneration, and tissue repair are not isolated problems; they share common threads in inflammation, microbial signaling, and immune dysregulation.

My first major research role as a Research Trainee at the National Institute of Immunology, New Delhi, placed me at the gut–brain interface — studying how the Triphala formulation modulates gut microbiota to confer neuroprotection in 5XFAD Alzheimer's mice. That work produced two publications and gave me a deep appreciation for how peripheral microbial signals drive central neuroinflammatory cascades.

I subsequently joined THSTI as a Junior Research Fellow, shifting focus to regenerative medicine — engineering biocompatible amniotic membrane scaffolds from human placental tissue for soft tissue repair, contributing to a publication in Advanced Biology.

I currently serve as a Senior Research Fellow at THSTI, now working on one of global medicine's most urgent problems: antimicrobial resistance. My project targets MDR and XDR Klebsiella pneumoniae through therapeutic monoclonal antibody discovery using hybridoma technology and in vivo validation.

Across all three chapters, the common thread has been translational intent — each project designed with a clear line of sight from bench observation to clinical application.
